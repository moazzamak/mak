Ark as the Internet of Blockchains

The internet revolution started with a small group of scientists who figured out how to connect computers separated by vast distances. This primitive version of what is now the internet was known at the time as ARPA-Net. The simple act of connecting computers into a single network has made such a great impact on society that no facet of our life today can work without it. In this post I will try to convince you that the same revolution is about to happen in the world of cryptocurrencies and that Ark is poised to lead it. I will also try to outline how we can connect multiple blockchains into a single internet of blockchains, let’s call it Arkanet, and how it’s consequences will cause disruption on the same scale as the internet did when it finally matured.

Introduction to ARK

  • Decentralization principles of crypto
    -Intends to have
    -No central control
    -No rent seekers, no tyrants
    -Run by the users for the users
    -Innovation happens at all nodes instead of center
    -Innovation can happen faster because there’s no human/beureaucratic bottleneck (linear vs exponential)
  • Ark’s Scaling philosophy
    -Single utility chains
    -Multiple chains connect to form an ecosystem
  • Smartbridges
    -Each transaction in the network can carry user provided data that everyone can see
    -These data fields can be used by special nodes to communicate information between different chains
  • Atomic swaps
    -Transferring value between different chains can also be done using special paired transactions called atomic swaps

Markets

  • Market fundamentals
    -Markets are a place for people to buy or sell assets
    -Price of any asset is determined by the balance of supply and demand of that asset
  • An analogy for supply and demand (tug of war)
    -This can be thought of as a tug of war contest between buyers and sellers of the asset
    -If there are more sellers than buyers then the price of the asset decreases until a balance between sellers and buyers is reached
    (demo simulation of supply/demand and price)
  • Liquidity and exponential price increases
    -Overall number of sellers and buyers determine the liquidity of the market
    (demo simulation of liquidity)
    -High liquidity markets are less volatile and more stable
     - Cryptocurrency market isn’t widely adopted enough to have high liquidity
     - Therefore cryptocurrency values are very volatile
    (demo simulation of volume and stability)
     - This creates a chicken and egg situation where people aren’t willing to use cryptocurrency because it’s volatile and cryptocurrency doesn’t become stable because there’s not enough people using it

Exchanges

  • The need for exchange
    -Exchanging one type of cryptocurrency for another will be a fundamental need especially since many cryptocurrencies intend to provide a unique utilities
  • Rent seeking intermediaries
     - Current exchanges seek exorbitant fees in order to list tokens
     - Serious projects have to setup a separate budget for getting on these exchanges
     - An additional requirement for getting on top exchanges is that the token should already have a sufficiently large trade volume
     - These conditions combine to make the crypto environment hostile to teams working on actual products and instead favours ponzi schemes that get on one exchange after another
    • If high liquidity could be achieved for each new project right out of the gate and the exchange system could be integrated as part of the protocol then this environment can be changed
